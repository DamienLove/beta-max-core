### Getting Started
1.  **Initialize**: Log in via the Portal or App.
2.  **Take the Test**: Complete the "Scout Qualification" exam to prove your technical aptitude.
3.  **Join a Mission**: Browse the "Available Betas" list.

### Operation Modes
You can interact with Beta Max in two ways:

#### 1. Visual Mode (GUI)
The standard graphical interface.
*   **Dashboard**: View your current rank, RP (Reputation Points), and active missions.
*   **Mission Card**: Tap to view details, download links, and known anomalies.

#### 2. Terminal Mode (CLI)
For elite users who prefer the command line. Access it via the "Terminal" tab.
*   `help`: List available commands.
*   `list missions`: Text-based list of active betas.
*   `scan`: Run a local device scan for anomalies.
*   `gui`: Switch back to Visual Mode.

### Submitting an Anomaly Log
When you find a bug:
1.  Navigate to the active Mission.
2.  Select **"Log Anomaly"**.
3.  **Severity**: Choose carefully (Cosmetic, Minor, Major, Critical).
4.  **Description**: Be precise. Vague reports earn zero RP.
5.  **Evidence**: Attach screenshots or logs (if supported).

## üë∑ For Architects (Developers)

### Registering a Mission
1.  Access the **Architect Portal**.
2.  Click **"New Operation"**.
3.  **Manifest Data**:
    *   **Codename**: Internal project name.
    *   **Public Title**: Display name.
    *   **Access Level**: Open Beta (Public) or Black Op (Private/Invite Only).
4.  **Integration**: Provide the package name (Android) or bundle ID (iOS) to allow the Beta Max app to detect installations.

### Managing Anomalies
*   **Review**: Check incoming logs in the "Inbox".
*   **Verify**: Mark logs as "Confirmed" to award RP to the Scout.
*   **Reject**: Mark as "Invalid" or "Duplicate" to penalize spam.
